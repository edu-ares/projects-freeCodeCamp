Marshal Cândido Mariano da Silva Rondon

Sertanista that acted in the integration of the west and northern Brazil and in defense of indigenous peoples.

  • 1864 His father, Cândido Mariano da Silva, Sr., was of Portuguese, Spanish, and Guaná (an indigenous group) ancestry, and died of smallpox, prior to Rondon's birth.
  • 1865 Born 1865 in Mimoso, a small village in the state of Mato Grosso.
  • 1867 His mother, Claudina Freitas Evangelista, descendent of the indigenous peoples Terena and Bororo.
  • 1880 Started teaching at an elementary school, after finishing high school at the age of 16.
  • 1881 Enrolled in the 3rd Regiment of Horse Artillery.
